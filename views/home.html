Lupercalia

In a completely historically accurate representation of Lupercalian festivities in Rome, a student pretends to be a wolf, seeking to break up pairs of lovers (talk about tough love...). As you can see here, everyone has an awesome time. Witness even more of the action in our 'Pictures' tab!

Ides of March

Our fearless leader dies, happy to be commemorating the Ides of March. While students are always willing to shed blood for TJ Latin, Twizzlers were selected as a tastier (and more tasteful) alternative.

Vergil's Birthday

On this oft-underrated holiday taking place on October 15, we pay homage to one of the greatest curriculum writers for AP Latin who ever lived with a reenactment of a baby's Lustratio, or naming ceremony.
